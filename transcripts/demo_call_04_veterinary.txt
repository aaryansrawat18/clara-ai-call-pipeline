[01:02] Sarah: What would you classify as a veterinary emergency?

[01:28] Sarah: How should emergency calls be handled?

[01:30] Dr. Foster: During clinic hours, transfer immediately to our triage nurse at extension 3. If the triage nurse doesn't answer in 15 seconds, transfer to the front desk at extension 1 — they can grab the nearest vet. After hours, this is important — we are NOT a 24-hour facility. The AI must tell them to go to Rocky Mountain Veterinary Emergency at 303-555-0199. That's our partner emergency hospital. Also take their info so we can follow up next business day. If we're within 30 minutes of opening, they can also wait and come in right when we open.

[02:05] Sarah: Very clear. Non-emergency calls during business hours?

[02:08] Dr. Foster: Transfer to front desk at extension 1 for appointment scheduling, general questions, prescription refills, and food orders. If the front desk is busy — give it 40 seconds — take a message. Name, pet's name, phone number, and reason for calling. We'll call back within 3 hours.
